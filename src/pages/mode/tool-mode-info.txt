2. SECTION: WHAT IS TOOL MODE?
------------------------------

[SECTION.WHAT_IS_TOOL_MODE.TITLE]
"What is Tool Mode?"

[SECTION.WHAT_IS_TOOL_MODE.BODY]
"Tool Mode is SSA’s way of honoring your current capacity. Instead of asking
you to design a whole stewardship system at once, it lets you work with one
tool, one flow, or one household area at a time.

In Tool Mode, you might:

- shape a cooking & meal rhythm,
- log your storehouse provisions,
- map a simple garden bed plan,
- define a cleaning & care cycle for one room,
- plan a single feast or gathering.

Each of these stands on its own and is fully useful. SSA quietly keeps your
data in structures that can later be woven into the broader household story."

[SECTION.WHAT_IS_TOOL_MODE.KEY_POINT_1.TITLE]
"One flow at a time"

[SECTION.WHAT_IS_TOOL_MODE.KEY_POINT_1.BODY]
"You focus on the rhythm in front of you: a meal pattern, a preservation session,
a room’s cleaning cycle. The page is not cluttered with other domains."

[SECTION.WHAT_IS_TOOL_MODE.KEY_POINT_2.TITLE]
"Season-aware, even when simple"

[SECTION.WHAT_IS_TOOL_MODE.KEY_POINT_2.BODY]
"Tool Mode can still show season hints and feast windows. You don’t have to
plan them; SSA simply reminds you of the time you are working in."

[SECTION.WHAT_IS_TOOL_MODE.KEY_POINT_3.TITLE]
"Ready to be woven later"

[SECTION.WHAT_IS_TOOL_MODE.KEY_POINT_3.BODY]
"Even in Tool Mode, your flows are saved in a way that can be connected to
storehouse, garden, animals, and feasts when you choose to step into
Stewardship Mode."


3. SECTION: WHY START IN TOOL MODE?
-----------------------------------

[SECTION.WHY_START.TITLE]
"Why would a householder start in Tool Mode?"

[SECTION.WHY_START.BODY]
"Households are often busy, stretched, or rebuilding. Tool Mode respects that.
It lets you begin with what your hands already touch: meals, dishes, laundry,
a bed, or the nearest shelf in the storehouse."

[SECTION.WHY_START.REASON_1.TITLE]
"To stay focused"

[SECTION.WHY_START.REASON_1.BODY]
"Instead of seeing every domain at once, Tool Mode lets you stay with one
area until it feels steady: a cooking rhythm, a cleaning flow, a garden bed."

[SECTION.WHY_START.REASON_2.TITLE]
"To avoid overwhelm"

[SECTION.WHY_START.REASON_2.BODY]
"You do not need to map the entire household structure to gain benefit.
You can begin with one small rhythm and grow as life allows."

[SECTION.WHY_START.REASON_3.TITLE]
"To build trust slowly"

[SECTION.WHY_START.REASON_3.BODY]
"As you use one tool and see how SSA handles your rhythms, seasons, and
storehouse information, you can decide if and when to connect more areas."

[SECTION.WHY_START.CALM_NOTE]
"It is completely acceptable for a householder to live in Tool Mode for a long
time. Stewardship Mode is an invitation, not a requirement."

never visit the full Stewardship overview."

8. SECTION: FAQ SNIPPETS
------------------------

[SECTION.FAQ.TITLE]
"Questions about Tool Mode"

[SECTION.FAQ.Q1.QUESTION]
"Do I lose anything by staying in Tool Mode?"

[SECTION.FAQ.Q1.ANSWER]
"No. Your flows, rhythms, and snapshots are fully useful on their own.
SSA stores them in a way that can be connected later if you choose Stewardship Mode."

[SECTION.FAQ.Q2.QUESTION]
"Can I switch between modes?"

[SECTION.FAQ.Q2.ANSWER]
"Yes. Mode is about how SSA presents information to you. Your underlying household
data remains the same. You can move between Tool Mode and Stewardship Mode as
your season of life changes."

[SECTION.FAQ.Q3.QUESTION]
"Do I have to set up every domain to use Tool Mode?"

[SECTION.FAQ.Q3.ANSWER]
"No. You can use just one tool for as long as you like. For example, you could
use only the Cooking & Meal Rhythm tool or only the Storehouse Snapshot tool."

[SECTION.FAQ.Q4.QUESTION]
"Does Tool Mode still use sessions and timers?"

[SECTION.FAQ.Q4.ANSWER]
"Yes. You can still start a SessionRunner cycle from within a tool to walk
through a flow step by step with timers, cues, and checkpoints."

[SECTION.FAQ.Q5.QUESTION]
"Is Tool Mode less ‘serious’ than Stewardship Mode?"

[SECTION.FAQ.Q5.ANSWER]
"Not at all. It’s simply a different way of walking: one step, one rhythm,
one area at a time. Some households may live in Tool Mode for years and that
is completely valid."
