0. PURPOSE
----------
This guide is a **practical language map** for SSA:

- How to talk about actions, lists, time, priorities, and collaboration
  in **householding terms**, not corporate productivity terms.
- How to keep language:
  - rooted in cycles, seasons, storehouse, yields, provisions, feasts, rhythms,
  - friendly and clear,
  - usable in TOOL MODE (single tool) and STEWARDSHIP MODE (connected web).

Use this when naming:
- buttons
- labels and headings
- empty states
- notifications
- onboarding / helper text


1. CORE STYLE SNAPSHOT
----------------------

1.1 We emphasize
- cycle, season, rhythm, flow
- storehouse, provision, yield
- feast, gathering
- householder, household
- care, tending, watching

1.2 We avoid
- task, todo, checklist, generic module
- productivity, optimize, workload, KPI
- project in the corporate sense

1.3 Tone anchors
- Calm, steady, practical.
- Encouraging but honest.
- Agrarian / Israelite flavor without quoting scripture:
  - storehouse, grain, oil, harvest, lean season, feast season.


2. COMMON REPLACEMENTS
----------------------

2.1 Generic → Household language

- “task”
  - → “rhythm”, “small flow”, “step in this cycle”

- “todo list”
  - → “rhythm list”, “today’s flows”, “household flows for this moment”

- “project”
  - → “household plan”, “longer cycle”, “seasonal flow”

- “module”
  - → “area”, “domain”, “household area”

- “resource”
  - → “provision”, “item in the storehouse”, “yield”

- “schedule”
  - → “rhythm”, “pattern”, “time window”

- “priority”
  - → “what needs the householder’s hands first”, “what this season is pressing”

- “deadline”
  - → “season window”, “feast day”, “before the lean season”, “before this gathering”

- “optimize”
  - → “steady”, “lighten strain”, “simplify”, “protect the householder”

- “workload”
  - → “load of rhythms”, “how heavy this cycle feels right now”


3. ACTION VERBS & BUTTON LABELS
-------------------------------

3.1 We like
- Start / Begin
- Shape / Adjust
- Steady / Lighten
- Mark / Note
- Log / Record
- Welcome (yields, guests)
- Share / Set aside
- Carry (storehouse, rhythms across seasons)

3.2 Examples

OLD → NEW

- “Add task”
  → “Add a small rhythm”
  → “Add a new flow for this area”

- “Create event”
  → “Mark a feast or gathering”
  → “Mark this day in your household calendar”

- “Create project”
  → “Shape a household plan for this season”

- “View tasks”
  → “View rhythms in this area”
  → “See flows for this week”

- “Edit module”
  → “Adjust this household area”
  → “Adjust this flow”

- “Delete task”
  → “Let this rhythm rest”
  → “Remove this flow from your map”


4. TIME & PRIORITY LANGUAGE
---------------------------

4.1 Time

Prefer:
- “this morning / today / this evening”
- “this week / this month”
- “this planting season / harvest season / lean season”
- “before the feast / after the feast”

Examples:
- “A feast is approaching in a few days.”
- “This is a planting window for cool-weather crops.”
- “Your storehouse is leaning; consider a small provisioning cycle this week.”

Avoid:
- “Due date”
- “Deadline”
- “Time slot” (unless absolutely necessary; prefer “time window”)

4.2 Priority

Prefer:
- “Most pressing for the household”
- “Needs the householder’s hands soon”
- “Can wait for a quieter moment”
- “Gentle first step”

Examples:
- “This storehouse category looks most pressing for the coming week.”
- “A gentle first step is to shape one small cleaning rhythm.”
- “This flow can wait for a calmer season.”

Avoid:
- “High priority / low priority” without context.
- “Critical issue” unless safety is actually at risk.


5. PROGRESS & COMPLETION
------------------------

5.1 Progress

Prefer:
- “A steady rhythm is forming here.”
- “This cycle has been carried through several weeks.”
- “This rhythm has been quiet for a while.”
- “You may be ready to deepen this flow.”

Avoid:
- “Level up”
- “Streak”
- “Achievement unlocked”
- “You completed 7 tasks!”

5.2 Completion

Prefer:
- “This flow is complete for now.”
- “You carried this rhythm through to the end.”
- “This session is finished; the rhythm remains available.”

Examples:
- Notification:
  - “This cooking session is complete. The rhythm is saved for next time.”
  - “You finished this cleaning cycle; rest is appropriate now.”

Avoid:
- “All tasks completed”
- “Inbox zero style language”


6. LISTS, QUEUES, & BOARDS
--------------------------

6.1 Lists

Prefer:
- “Rhythms in this area”
- “Flows planned for today”
- “Sessions ready to run”
- “Storehouse entries”

Examples:
- “Household flows for this week”
- “Preservation sessions waiting on yields”

6.2 Queues

Prefer:
- “Queue” → “line of rhythms”, “line of sessions”, “upcoming flows”

Examples:
- “Upcoming cooking flows”
- “Preservation flows waiting for this harvest”

Avoid:
- “Backlog”
- “Ticket queue”

6.3 Boards/Kanban

If you need a board-style metaphor, use:
- “Stages in this rhythm”
- “States of this flow”
- “From ‘waiting’ to ‘in motion’ to ‘resting’”

Example column labels:
- “Not yet started” → “Not yet in motion”
- “In progress” → “In motion”
- “Done” → “Completed for now” or “At rest”


7. COLLABORATION & ROLES
------------------------

7.1 People

Use:
- “householder” for the primary responsible person.
- “helpers” or “family members” for others.
- “household” for the group.

Examples:
- “The householder can adjust this rhythm at any time.”
- “Helpers can see and join flows assigned to this room.”

Avoid:
- “Owner” in UI (keep that notion internal if needed).
- “Assignee” – use “who tends this rhythm” or “who is watching this flow”.

7.2 Assigning & Sharing

Prefer:
- “Invite someone to share this rhythm.”
- “Mark who tends this flow.”
- “Share this session with another household.”

Examples:
- “Mark who tends this rhythm”
- “Share this provisioning plan with your mutual-aid circle”

Avoid:
- “Assign task to user”
- “Reassign ticket”


8. ERROR & ALERT LANGUAGE
-------------------------

8.1 Errors

Patterns:
- State what went wrong, then how to recover.
- Keep the tone calm and practical.

Examples:
- “Something interrupted this flow. Please try again.”
- “SSA couldn’t save this rhythm right now. Check your connection, then try again.”
- “A required field is missing. Fill in the highlighted sections so this flow can be recorded.”

Avoid:
- “Fatal error”
- “Operation failed” without explanation.

8.2 Alerts & Warnings

Tie to season, storehouse, or rhythm, not guilt.

Examples:
- “This pattern may be drawing too heavily from the storehouse.”
- “A season has shifted; this rhythm may need a lighter expression.”
- “A feast is approaching; provisions in this category look tight.”

Avoid:
- “You’re behind”
- “You failed to complete your tasks”


9. EMPTY STATES & ONBOARDING
----------------------------

9.1 Empty State Patterns

Patterns:
- Acknowledge emptiness.
- Offer a gentle first step.
- Optional second step for more advanced.

Generic template:
- Title: “Begin with one simple rhythm.”
- Body: “This area is empty. Start with one small flow you already do.”
- CTA primary: “Name your first rhythm”
- CTA secondary: “Browse example flows”

Examples:
- “No storehouse records yet. Begin by logging what you already have on hand.”
- “No garden beds recorded yet. Start by naming one bed or planting space.”
- “No preservation flows are active. When a harvest or bulk purchase arrives, this panel will help you choose a preservation rhythm.”

Avoid:
- “No data yet. Add some tasks to get started.”
- “You haven’t set up anything. Click here to configure.”


10. TOOL MODE VS STEWARDSHIP MODE LANGUAGE
------------------------------------------

10.1 TOOL MODE

Goals:
- Make the user feel safe to work locally.
- Gently hint at future integration.

Patterns:
- “This page works on its own.”
- “You’re shaping one household rhythm.”
- “SSA can link this flow with your seasons and storehouse when you’re ready.”

Examples:
- “Today’s view focuses on a single flow. You can still see how it fits your seasons and storehouse.”
- “This tool is a focused flow. As you get comfortable, SSA can link it with your other rhythms.”

10.2 STEWARDSHIP MODE

Goals:
- Emphasize connection and overview.
- Show cross-domain relationships.

Patterns:
- “This dashboard weaves your cooking, storehouse, garden, preservation, cleaning, and feasts into one stewardship view.”
- “These panels show how your cycles, seasons, and yields are working together.”

Examples:
- “Connected stewardship is active. This overview shows how your rhythms and provisions align with this season.”
- “These panels reveal which areas are steady, which are quiet, and which may need the householder’s attention.”


11. SHORT TEMPLATE LIBRARY
--------------------------

Use these as seeds when writing new copy.

11.1 Rhythm / Flow Templates
- “Shape a [domain] rhythm that fits this season and your storehouse.”
- “This flow helps you carry [provision or area] across the coming weeks.”
- “A gentle way to begin is to start with one [daily/weekly/seasonal] rhythm.”

11.2 Storehouse Templates
- “Your storehouse is leaning in [category]. A small provisioning cycle may help.”
- “This preservation run will help your storehouse carry you through the next season.”
- “At your current rhythm, this storehouse category holds about [X] weeks of meals.”

11.3 Season & Feast Templates
- “A season has shifted; some rhythms may need adjusting.”
- “A feast is approaching; review your storehouse and meal rhythms to welcome it without strain.”
- “This is a rich season; preserved yields now will steady the lean period ahead.”

11.4 Insight Templates
- “A steady rhythm is forming here; you may be ready to deepen it.”
- “This rhythm has been quiet for a while; if it’s no longer needed, you can let it rest.”
- “Garden and storehouse patterns suggest this is a good time for a small preservation flow.”
